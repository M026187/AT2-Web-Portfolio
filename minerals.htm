10 Most significant minerals mined in Western Australia

  • Iron ore: Rocks and minerals from which metallic iron can be economically extracted.
  • Petroleum: Meaning rock oil is a naturally occurring, oily flammable liquid composed mainly of hydrocarbons, formed from the remains of ancient organisms.
  • Gold: Chemical element with the symbol Au. In its pure form, it is a bright, slightly orange-yellow, dense, soft, malleable and ductile metal.
  • Lithium: Chemical element with the symbol Li. It is a soft, silvery-white alkali metal.
  • Alumina: Also known as aluminium oxide, is a white, granular material, refined from bauxite and is the key material for producing aluminium.
  • Nickel: Chemical element with the symbol Ni. It is a silvery-white lustrous metal with a slight gold tinge.
  • Copper: Chemical element with the symbol Cu. It is a soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity.
  • Zinc: Chemical element with the symbol Zn. It is a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.
  • Salt: A chemical compound (electrolyte) made up of sodium and chloride.
